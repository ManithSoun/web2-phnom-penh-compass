Do’s & Don’ts

Navigate Phnom Penh with Respect and Confidence

Greet with a “Sampeah”

Put palms together and bow slightly to show respect.

Learn simple Khmer phrases

Locals appreciate small efforts like “Hello” or “Thank you”.

Remove shoes before entering homes

Always take shoes off when entering someone's house.

Show respect to elders & monks

Greet politely and avoid standing higher than monks.

Dress modestly at temples

Cover shoulders & knees when visiting religious sites.

Dispose trash properly

Help keep Phnom Penh clean by using bins.

Wear a helmet when riding motos

Safety first — and it's required by law.

Negotiate politely

Bargaining is normal — just keep it friendly.

Carry small cash

Many local shops only accept small bills.

Don't touch anyone’s head

The head is considered sacred in Khmer culture.

Don't point feet at people

Feet are seen as unclean — avoid pointing or touching.

Don't touch monks (especially women)

Physical contact with monks is inappropriate.

Don't litter in public

Keep the city clean and dispose trash properly.

Don't raise your voice

Loud or aggressive tone is considered rude.

Don't enter temples with shoes

Always remove shoes at temples and sacred places.

Don't show excessive PDA

Public affection is not culturally accepted.

Don't touch sacred objects

Always ask before touching religious items.

Don't show anger in public

Staying calm is a sign of respect in Khmer culture.
